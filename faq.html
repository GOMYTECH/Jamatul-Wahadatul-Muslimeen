About Jamatul Wahadatul Muslimeen

Our mission is to promote Islamic unity, provide authentic Islamic education, and serve the Muslim community through spiritual, educational, and social programs based on the Quran and Sunnah.

Jamatul Wahadatul Muslimeen was founded in 1995 by a group of dedicated Muslim brothers under the leadership of Amir Taiwo Lasisi.

You can join us by completing our membership registration form available at any of our centers or through our website. We welcome all Muslims who share our vision of Islamic unity and community development.

We offer various programs including:

  • Daily prayers and Jumu'ah services
  • Islamic education for children and adults
  • Youth development programs
  • Marriage counseling and family services
  • Charitable activities and community support
  • Dawah and interfaith dialogue

We are funded through donations from our members and well-wishers, as well as through income from our halal business ventures. All financial matters are handled with transparency according to Islamic principles.

Islamic Practices

The five pillars of Islam are:

  1. Shahada (Declaration of Faith)
  2. Salah (Prayer five times daily)
  3. Zakat (Obligatory charity)
  4. Sawm (Fasting during Ramadan)
  5. Hajj (Pilgrimage to Makkah if able)

The proper steps for wudu are:

  1. Make intention in your heart
  2. Say "Bismillah"
  3. Wash hands up to wrists 3 times
  4. Rinse mouth 3 times
  5. Sniff water into nostrils and blow out 3 times
  6. Wash face from hairline to chin 3 times
  7. Wash arms up to elbows 3 times (right first)
  8. Wipe head with wet hands (once)
  9. Wipe inside and outside of ears (once)
  10. Wash feet up to ankles 3 times (right first)

We offer classes at our centers for proper demonstration.

While Zakat is obligatory, Islam highly encourages voluntary charity (Sadaqah). The Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) said: "Charity does not decrease wealth" (Muslim). We recommend:

  • Regular small donations
  • Sponsoring orphans or students
  • Contributing to community projects
  • Giving in secret when possible
  • Even a smile is considered charity

Balancing deen and dunya is achievable with:

  • Establishing a consistent prayer schedule
  • Creating a Quran reading routine (even few verses daily)
  • Choosing halal entertainment alternatives
  • Building relationships with pious friends
  • Attending Islamic programs regularly
  • Seeking Islamic knowledge during commute (audio lectures)

Our organization offers time management workshops specifically for Muslims.

Welcome to Islam! We recommend these first steps:

  1. Learn how to pray (start with basics, don't overwhelm yourself)
  2. Connect with a local Muslim community
  3. Begin reading Quran with translation
  4. Learn about Prophet Muhammad's (PBUH) life
  5. Take things gradually - Islam is a journey

We have special programs for new Muslims at all our centers.
