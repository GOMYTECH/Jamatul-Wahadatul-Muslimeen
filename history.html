About Jamatul Wahadatul Muslimeen

Discover our history, mission, and the principles that guide our Islamic organization

Our History

Jamatul Wahadatul Muslimeen (JWM) was established in 1995 by a group of dedicated Muslim brothers who shared a common vision of Islamic unity and community development.

1995

Foundation

Founded by Amir Taiwo Lasisi and a group of brothers to promote Islamic unity and education in Nigeria.

2001

First Islamic Center

Established our first permanent Islamic center for prayers, education, and community gatherings.

2008

Youth Programs

Launched comprehensive youth development programs to engage young Muslims.

2015

Expansion

Expanded operations to multiple states online with new community projects.

2023

Current Status

Now serving communities across Nigeria with educational, charitable, and dawah programs.

Mission & Vision

Our Mission

To promote the teachings of Islam, encourage unity among Muslims, and engage in community development through education, charity, and advocacy based on Quran and Sunnah.

Our Vision

To be a leading Islamic organization that fosters spiritual growth, social harmony, and sustainable development while maintaining Islamic values and principles.

Our Values

Unity, Justice, Knowledge, Service, and Peaceful Coexistence guide all our activities and decisions.

Core Beliefs & Principles

Our organization is founded on these fundamental Islamic principles:

Tawheed (Oneness of Allah)

Upholding the absolute oneness of God as the foundation of Islamic belief.

Unity of the Ummah

Promoting brotherhood among Muslims beyond ethnic or sectarian differences.

Quran & Sunnah

Following the Quran and authentic teachings of Prophet Muhammad (PBUH).

Justice & Equity

Advocating for fairness and justice in all aspects of society.

Education

Prioritizing Islamic and secular knowledge for personal and community development.

Service to Humanity

Serving all people regardless of faith through charitable works.
